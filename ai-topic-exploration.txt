Best Analogy Or Example
Think of a modern GPU as a throughput engine: it is not built to make one task finish with the lowest possible latency, like a high-end CPU core. It is built to keep thousands of tiny operations moving at once, especially when those operations are similar and run over large arrays of pixels, vertices, particles, matrix elements, or neural-network activations.
